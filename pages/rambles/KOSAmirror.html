What is KOSA?

KOSA, or the Kids Online Safety Act is a bill that proposes mandatory ID age verification for anything your attorney general deems is inappropriate for kids. What the bill decides is "unsafe" is extremely vague and covers a wide range of topics(listed below) which can inadvertently (or not, some states governments are assholes) harm kids.

What will happen if this is passed federally or in all states?

If this bill is passed, it is a major blow to basic human rights like freedom of speech, privacy and beliefs.
Sites that have any of these will likely require users to input government issued ID:

  • Adult entertainment
  • Sexual health and reproductive rights information
  • History of marginalized groups
  • Help for mental health
  • Help for addiction recovery

When I say "18+ information", I am referring to anything that meets this list EXCEPT for adult entertainment.
It will also force companies that make at least $2.5 billion USD in annual revenue to heavily censor any content that can have even the slightest chance of causing self-harm, suicide, eating disorders, substance abuse, depression, anxiety, and bullying, among other things. While considered a small improvement, most online speech happens on platforms like Meta, X, Snapchat, etc, all of which make at least $2.5 billion USD in annual revenue or more. Smaller companies are not subject to this part of KOSA, however "they can just use another provision of KOSA."
It will also hurt any adults who do not want to give up their privacy and anonymity online. Not only that, it has the potential to increase the amount of identity theft as people want to access these services without giving up their identity.
There is simply no upside to this bill.
